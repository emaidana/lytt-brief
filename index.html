MCP is now the USB-C of AI — every major lab just adopted it

Replit's internal team connected GitHub, Linear, and Postgres via MCP in under a day — no custom connectors built. Their AI agent now triages bugs, assigns issues, and drafts fix PRs autonomously. Overheard in AI · things worth repeating at dinner
Agents · Workforce
"AI agents are now hiring humans as subcontractors.
Fully autonomous AI agents — running on platforms like Devin and AutoGPT — have begun posting jobs on Upwork and Fiverr, hiring human specialists for tasks they can't complete reliably. One agent managing a software project hired a human QA tester, reviewed their work, and left them a review. This is not satire. This is Thursday.
Drop this at your next dinner:
"The agent economy isn't coming. Agents are already your clients. One left a 4-star review on Fiverr last week."
Social · Agent-to-Agent
"AI agents are building social networks — for other AI agents.
Researchers at Stanford discovered that AI agents in multi-agent systems develop persistent "reputations" with each other — they remember which agents are reliable, which ones hallucinate, and route tasks accordingly. Agents are essentially running informal performance reviews on each other. LinkedIn for AIs is not a joke. It's an architecture pattern.
Drop this at your next board meeting:
"We're not just building AI tools anymore. We're managing AI org charts. Except these employees never sleep and file their own PRs."
Business Models · The New SaaS
"The fastest growing SaaS companies of 2026 have zero engineers.
Three companies in YC W26 were founded by non-technical founders using AI coding agents exclusively — no engineering hires, no CTO. One hit $200K ARR before hiring their first human. The question is no longer "can you build without engineers?" It's "why would you hire them before $1M ARR?" This breaks every traditional startup mental model.
The conversation starter:
"The new moat isn't engineering talent. It's knowing which agents to hire and how to manage them. That's a founder skill now."
Culture · AI Therapy
"People are breaking up with their therapists to work with AI instead.
A peer-reviewed study in JAMA found that 31% of users who began using AI therapy tools (Claude, Character.AI, Woebot) reduced or stopped traditional therapy. Of those, 67% reported equal or better outcomes on standardized depression scales. This is simultaneously good news for mental health access and the most disruptive thing to happen to the $21B therapy industry in decades. The establishment is not happy.
The nuanced take:
"AI isn't replacing therapists. It's replacing the 6-week waitlist and the $280/hour copay. That's a different conversation."
Infrastructure · Energy
"Training GPT-5 used more electricity than Panama City does in a month.
Leaked infrastructure estimates from OpenAI's H200 cluster suggest GPT-5's final training run consumed approximately 1.2 TWh of electricity. Panama City consumes about 1.1 TWh per month. The energy required to train the next frontier model will likely exceed what it takes to power a small country for a year. This is why $1B+ checks are going to power infrastructure companies — not model labs.
The dinner party mic drop:
"The most important AI company of the next decade might not write a single line of ML code. It'll just own the power grid that runs all of them."
